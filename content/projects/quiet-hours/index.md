For years I shipped things that fought for the foreground — badges, pings, that little red dot. Quiet Hours started as a question: what would an app look like if it assumed your attention was *borrowed*, not owned?

The answer, mostly, was **less**. No notifications unless you asked. No streaks to protect. A home screen that was happy to be empty.

It never became a product. But it became a lens I still build through — the quiet was the whole point.
