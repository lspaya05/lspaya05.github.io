I started keeping a notebook because I kept having the same thought twice and recognizing it the *second* time. Writing it down once was cheaper.

The trick was lowering the cost of an entry to almost nothing. No folders, no tags up front — just a line and a timestamp. Structure can come later, if it comes at all.

> The messy middle is not a failure of the system. It **is** the system.

Most entries are bad. A few aren't. You can't tell which in advance, which is the whole argument for writing all of them down.
